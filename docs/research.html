Research

Research Overview

My research focuses on efficient large language model training on heterogeneous CPU-GPU systems, distributed machine learning, and high-performance computing. I am particularly interested in optimizing resource utilization and reducing training time for large-scale AI workloads. At IISc, I designed a hybrid CPU-GPU training framework that achieved up to 17% reduction in training time for models like Llama2 and GPT2 compared to existing state-of-the-art approaches. My work involves developing novel scheduling strategies, implementing producer-consumer pipelines, and integrating with PyTorch's Distributed Data Parallel framework.

Research Projects

Joint Resource Allocation and Service Migration in Vehicular Edge Networks

2023 - 2024

Formulated a joint MDP for network-initiated resource allocation and service migration across MEC servers. Proposed a modified DDPG-based solution achieving 26.67% reduction in service violations.

Deep Reinforcement Learning Edge Computing Vehicular Networks

TLCC: Accelerated Connected Component Analysis via Two-Stage Parallelism

2023

Designed a two-layer CCA using OR-mask filtering and parallel region-wise CCA, achieving 64.37% reduction in CPU simulation time over traditional CCA.

Computer Vision Parallel Computing Image Processing

PAC-IPV: Prefetch-Aware Cache Replacement via Insertion & Promotion Vectors

2023

Implemented prefetch-aware cache replacement policy extending RRIP-based LLC in ZSim. Developed probabilistic Markov-based analytical model achieving performance competitive to SHiP at lower complexity.
